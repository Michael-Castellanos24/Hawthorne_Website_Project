Welcome!

Welcome to Hawthorne, New Jersey! A Passaic County town located just north of Paterson across the Passaic River. You'll find many different places here, such as Goffle Brook Park, our many public school that our student residents attend, our many churches, and a hill with a great view of the New York City skyline! Be sure to grab a delicious slice of pizza from one of our many pizzerias in town. Or, if you're in town at the right time, attend one of our town events, such as our annual Hawthorne Day, or our annual Christmas Tree lighting and parade! Theres a lot of places to see in Hawthorne, so we hope you enjoy your visit!

Additional Information

Hawthorne has several town events which are held throughout the year. These include the Memorial Day Parade, the Saint Anthony's Feast held in June, Hawthorne Day held in September, and the Christmas Tree lighting and parade held in December. Hawthorne has multiple forms of public transportation from New Jersey Transit in the form of bus and commuter rail. The NJ Transit Main Line is a commuter rail line that runs seven days a week. Hawthorne has it's own train station where commuters can board a train. The train takes commuters directly to Hoboken to the south, and Suffern, NY to the north. Commuters can also take the train to midtown New York City, by transfering at the Secaucus Junction station. The 722 Bus runs through Hawthorne, which can take passengers to Paterson or the Paramus Park Mall. The bus runs only on Mondays through Fridays, and during rush hours only. Commute times into New York City from Hawthorne is also relitivly short, with drives into Manhattan usually being less than an hour. However traffic could make these commute times longer.
